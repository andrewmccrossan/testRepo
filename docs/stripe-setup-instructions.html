Setting Up Stripe to Sell Your Books & Cards

A step-by-step guide — no technical knowledge needed

What we're doing, and what I need at the end

Stripe is the service that will collect payments from your customers and deposit the money into your bank account. By the end of this guide you'll have created one payment link (a web address) for each book you want to sell, plus one payment link for your greeting-card pack.

All I need from you   Copy each of those links into the table at the end and send them back to me. I'll connect them to the website — you won't have to touch any code.

Set aside about 30–45 minutes. You can stop partway and pick up later; Stripe saves your progress. Everything here assumes you're in the United States and selling in US dollars — tell me if that's not the case and I'll adjust.

Part 1 · Create your Stripe account

This is a one-time setup. Stripe is a large, regulated payments company (the same one used by thousands of small businesses), so it will ask you to verify who you are and where to send your money.

  1. In your web browser, go to stripe.com and click Sign in (top right), then Create account.
  2. Enter your email address, your full name, and a password you'll remember. Choose United States as the country. Click to create the account.
  3. Stripe sends you a confirmation email. Open it and click the link inside to verify your email address.
  4. Now you'll activate the account so it can take real payments. Stripe will ask you for:
    • Your legal name, home address, and date of birth
    • The last four digits of your Social Security Number (used only to confirm your identity)
    • A short description of what you sell — you can write: “Photography books and greeting cards about the art and churches of Italy.”
    • Your bank account and routing number, so Stripe can deposit your sales
    When it asks whether you're a business or an individual, choosing Individual is the simplest option for an author selling their own work.
  5. It may ask for a statement descriptor — this is the short name that appears on your customer's credit-card bill. Set it to something they'll recognize, like ITALYBYGREGPULLES or GREG PULLES BOOKS.
Good to know Stripe doesn't charge a monthly fee. It takes a small cut of each sale — about 2.9% plus 30¢ per transaction in the US (so on a $30 order, roughly $1.17). You keep the rest. There's nothing to pay up front.

Part 2 · A few settings before you start

Make sure you're in "live" mode, not "test" mode

Stripe has a practice area called Test mode. Links made there can't take real money. Look for a Test mode switch near the top right of the dashboard and make sure it is turned OFF before you create your final links. (If you'd like to do a practice run first, you're welcome to — just remember the real links must be made with Test mode off.)

If you get stuck here If Stripe says your account isn't fully activated yet, finish the activation questions from Part 1 first. Real payment links only work once Stripe has approved your account. This can take a few minutes to a day.

Part 3 · Add your books — one link each

You'll repeat these steps once per book you want to sell directly. Each book gets its own link.

  1. In the menu on the left, click Payment links. If you don't see it, type “Payment links” into the search box at the very top of the page and choose it.
  2. Click Create payment link (or the + New button). Choose Products when asked what kind of link.
  3. Click + Add a product and fill in:
    • Name — the book's title, e.g. Sacred Places: Rediscovering the Churches of Rome
    • Price — what you want to charge (e.g. 75.00)
    • Image (optional) — upload a photo of the cover so customers see it at checkout
    Make sure it's a one-time payment (not a subscription).
  4. Because books are shipped, you must collect the buyer's address. Open the Options (sometimes under “More options” or a “Shipping” / “Collect addresses” section) and turn Collect shipping address to ON.
  5. (Optional) If you want to charge for shipping, add a shipping rate (a flat amount like 6.00). Otherwise you can simply build shipping into the price.
  6. Click Create link. Stripe shows you a web address that starts with https://buy.stripe.com/. Click Copy.
  7. Paste that link into the books table at the end next to the matching title. Then repeat from step 2 for your next book.
Tip You don't have to sell every book through the website. Make a link only for the ones you want customers to buy direct. (You can still list the others with an “Available on Amazon” link — just tell me which.)

Part 4 · Add your greeting cards — ONE link for the whole pack

The most important point in this guide Do NOT make a separate link for each card. Customers build a pack of 12 on the website by choosing from your photos. Stripe only needs one product — “a pack of 12 cards” — at one price. The website remembers which 12 they picked and passes that along automatically.
  1. Click Payment links → Create payment link → Products, just like before.
  2. Add one product:
    • Name: Photographic Greeting Cards — Pack of 12
    • Price: the price for a full pack of 12 (e.g. 30.00)
    • Image (optional): a photo showing a few sample cards
  3. Turn Collect shipping address to ON (the cards are mailed), exactly as you did for the books.
  4. If there's an option about letting the customer adjust the quantity, leave it OFF / set to 1. (One link = one pack of 12. A customer who wants two packs can simply check out twice.)
  5. Click Create link, then Copy the https://buy.stripe.com/ address and paste it into the cards row of the table at the end.

How you'll know which photos to print

When a card order comes in, it carries a short list of codes for the 12 photos the customer chose — something like FOUNTAIN1×5, STATUE1×7 — meaning 5 of one photo and 7 of another. You don't need to memorize or decode anything. For your first few orders, just forward me the order and I'll tell you exactly which photographs to print, and I'll show you where that list appears in your Stripe dashboard so you can read it yourself going forward.

Common questions

Where do I see my orders?

In the Stripe dashboard, click Payments in the left menu. Every sale appears there with the customer's name, what they bought, and their shipping address.

When do I get paid?

Stripe deposits your sales into your bank account automatically. The first deposit usually takes about a week while Stripe verifies everything; after that, money arrives every couple of business days.

Can I change a price later?

Yes. You can edit a product's price in Stripe at any time — the same link keeps working. If you change the card pack price, just let me know so I can update the price shown on the website to match.

What about sales tax?

Stripe offers an optional feature called Stripe Tax that can calculate it for you. It's not required to get started — we can turn it on later if you'd like. Bring it up with me and we'll decide together.

What if a customer wants a refund?

You can refund any payment with a couple of clicks from the Payments page in Stripe. The fee is returned to you on refunds.
